The fractional AI officer: when a full-time hire doesn't make sense yet

By Reckona AIUpdated 23 July 20267 min read

Somewhere between "the founder reads AI Twitter and decides on projects ad hoc" and "we have a full Chief AI Officer with a headcount budget" sits most mid-sized companies — genuinely needing senior AI judgment, without enough ongoing decision volume to justify a full-time executive salary for it. That gap is exactly what a fractional AI officer fills.

What the role actually does

Not a consultant who writes a strategy deck and leaves. A fractional AI officer functions as a part-time, embedded executive: they sit in on the same planning conversations a full-time hire would, but for a defined number of days a month rather than five. In practice, that means:

  • Prioritising the AI roadmap against actual business metrics, not against whatever the market is hyped about that quarter.
  • Vetting build-vs-buy decisions — whether a use case needs a custom system or an off-the-shelf tool already solves it well enough.
  • Owning vendor and governance judgment — reviewing what data an AI vendor touches, what a contract actually commits you to, and whether a proposed system meets your compliance bar.
  • Being the internal translator between technical implementers and non-technical leadership, so decisions don't get made either by people who don't understand the tradeoffs or by people with no business context.

How to tell which stage you're at

Three questions, honestly answered, usually settle it:

  1. How many AI-related decisions does your leadership team make per month that actually need senior technical-plus-business judgment? Fewer than 4-5 — a project consultant per initiative is enough. Roughly 5-15 — fractional fits well. Constant, cross-functional — you likely need full-time.
  2. Is there already someone internal who could own this, given senior air cover? If yes, a fractional AI officer's real job may be mentoring that person into the role over 6-12 months, not holding it indefinitely.
  3. What's the cost of a bad AI vendor decision to your company right now? If it's large (compliance exposure, customer data, a six-figure contract), the governance judgment alone often justifies a fractional retainer even before counting the roadmap work.

The honest test: if you can't name a single AI decision in the last quarter that would have gone differently with senior oversight, you may not need this role yet — a project-based engagement per initiative is the leaner fit.

How this connects to execution

A fractional AI officer sets direction and makes judgment calls; it doesn't replace the people who actually build the systems.
