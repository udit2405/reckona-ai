What GEO actually is

A buyer asks an AI engine a question and gets a synthesized answer citing two or three sources. Nobody scans a results page; nobody scrolls. You win by being the source the engine retrieves, quotes and names — which depends on entity clarity, quotable structure, citable data and third-party corroboration far more than on your position for any single keyword. That is a different discipline from classic SEO, and it is where the early-mover advantage still exists.

The one-sentence case: rankings won't protect you from being omitted or mis-described in AI answers — and in most Indian mid-market categories, that's exactly what's already happening.

Common questions

What is Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)?

Getting your brand cited, recommended and correctly described inside AI answer engines — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews — rather than merely ranked on a traditional results page. It depends on entity clarity, quotable structured content, citable data and cross-source corroboration.

How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO wins a position on a results page a human clicks. GEO wins a citation inside a synthesized AI answer where nobody scrolls. They share clean technical foundations, but optimizing only for rankings leaves the answer layer to competitors. More on this in SEO isn't dying, it's splitting in two.

How do you measure GEO results?

A monthly visibility panel: a 40-prompt matrix across four AI engines, scored for whether your brand is mentioned, recommended and described accurately, benchmarked against competitors' share of voice.

Do you guarantee we'll be recommended by AI?

No honest firm guarantees a specific engine output. We guarantee process, measurement, and movement on agreed leading indicators by day 90 — proven against your baseline panel.
